# Game Design Principles Overview
Some common game design principles include **player feedback**, **meaningful choice**, **idle or progression systems**, and **reward balance**. These ideas are often used in both casual and competitive games. 
For example, *Slayer Legend* applies meaningful choice through skill combinations and character builds that let players approach battles differently. Its idle progression system allows players to keep earning rewards even when offline, encouraging long-term engagement without constant attention. This balance between activity and reward is what keeps many mobile players motivated. # One Principle That Resonates with Me
The principle that resonates most with me is the **idle or AFK progression system**. In *Slayer Legend*, players continue to gain experience and loot even while offline. 
I like this idea because it respects the player’s time and keeps them feeling connected to the game’s growth loop. 
It’s a clever way to make progress feel continuous and rewarding without forcing endless grinding. Player can easily collect the reward after a long work shift, they won't feel left behind even if they don't have enough time to online.
If I design my own game, I’d include a simple version of this principle—maybe letting players accumulate small rewards or points over time—so they always feel a sense of achievement even during breaks.
